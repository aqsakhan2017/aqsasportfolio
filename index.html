About Me

My name is Aqsa. I'm a 17 year old senior who studies at Bard High School Early College Queens. I developed a love for writing at an early age, and saw myself doing that as a career. I spent hours writing everything from weekly family newspapers to Harry Potter fanfiction.

I'm still deeply in love with writing, however have found new passions along the way. Over the summer of 2016, I was a part of an empowering program called the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion program (GWCSIP). I had entered thinking it would be a fun experience and it proved not only to be a fun and exciting experience, but one that taught me a lot about networking, leadership skills, the importance of girls in STEM profession, and most importantly 5 different coding languages. Including Python (and PyGames), Arduino, HTML, CSS, and Javascript; I've self taught myself Swift and am learning Java in a class. I'm actively learning more languages as well as strengthening my skills in the languages I already do know. One day, I hope to inspire other girls (and others in general) to learn to code and change the world for the better.

Computer Programming

Languages: Python, PyGames, Java, HTML, CSS, Javascript, Swift, Arduino

This is the final project that I worked on during Girls Who Code. It is a website that works like an Instagram fused with Pinterest. You can upload pictures of your own and save them to either a food board or fashion board. When you click on a picture on the food board, a recipe will pop up. If you click on a picture on the fashion board, the stores that have the clothes that each model is wearing will be listed. There is also a functioning login/logout.

Using Python, I reassigned the color values to create an obamicon of an internet celebrity, Lilly Singh.

Using Java, I created a fully functioning tic tac toe game.

During the Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program, my group successfully programmed our robot and dealt with circuitry to make our robot do many things like do a dance complete with a song (using a piezo) and blinking LED's as well as used whiskers and built an obstacle course for the robot.
